សិប្បកម្មសូត្រខ្មែរ Khmer Silk Craft

Khmer Silk Craft is an extraordinary artistic heritage with a history spanning over a thousand years, deeply rooted in the beliefs, religion, and national identity of Cambodia since the Angkorian era. What makes it globally renowned is the use of native "Golden Silk" combined with the intricate "Chong Kiet" (Ikat) technique—a sophisticated process requiring immense precision to tie-dye patterns onto threads before the weaving even begins. Beyond being a luxury textile, Khmer silk stands as a symbol of the patience and creative brilliance of Cambodian women, who have preserved this ancestral wisdom to remain a source of national pride and a rising star in the world of international fashion.

ប្រភពសូត្រខ្មែរ Khmer Silk Origin

The origin of Khmer silk craft dates back over a thousand years to the Angkorian era, where it emerged as a sacred art form used for royal garments and temple offerings. Utilizing the native Golden Silkworm and the intricate "Chong Kiet" (Ikat) tie-dye technique, Khmer ancestors developed a unique style of weaving that became a cornerstone of Cambodian identity. Despite the threat of extinction in the late 20th century, the craft was preserved by resilient master weavers, ensuring that today's silk remains a living bridge to Cambodia’s glorious past.

ចក្ខុវិស័យ និងបេសកកម្ម Vision and Mission

Our vision is to elevate Khmer silk craft to the global stage as a symbol of elegance, heritage, and cultural pride. We aim to preserve the traditional art of handwoven silk while inspiring modern fashion that reflects Cambodia’s identity,creativity, and timeless craftsmanship.
Our mission is to preserve and promote the ancient Khmer silk weaving tradition by supporting local artisans, using sustainable methods, and transforming heritage patterns into contemporary designs. We are committed to keeping Cambodian craftsmanship alive while sharing its beauty with the world.
